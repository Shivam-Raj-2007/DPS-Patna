PARENTAL WORKSHOP


Language development is an important aspect in a child’s overall development. It supports a child’s ability to communicate, to express and understand feelings, to think and learn, to solve problems, and also to develop and maintain relationships. It is understood that for our children to be successful in life, they need support and guidance at both home and at school. A strong partnership between parents and teachers can make a significant difference in their education as being partners and both parents and teachers share the responsibility for children’s success. Keeping this view in mind a virtual parent’s workshop on the topic ‘Language Development in Young Learners’ was conducted on 8th January 2022.

Sections were clubbed and two teachers took session in one group. The workshop flagged off with a take-off question asked to parents that, if they recall any memorable incident from their childhood memory related to learning or improving language?A blind fold activity was conducted, where parents were asked to draw a snowman followed by other instructions. It was a kind of ice breaking session but at the same time the teacher emphasized how it can help in brainstorming. Also, the different stages of development of language were explained. There are many activities that integrate language learning with fun such as: Riddles, Rhymes and Homonyms, Storytelling, Songs and tongue twisters. For enhancing listening and speaking skills, a memory game was conducted where parents were asked to take the name of any animal (as our theme for the month is animals). Then next person will first recall the name taken by the last person and then add one new name to it. In this same way the chain continued. Also, Show and Tell and Word Antakshari were taken up for enhancing speaking and listening skills, observational skills and developing vocabulary.

For the development of writing skills, activities were conducted such as, See and Write where parents were shown a picture and were asked to write the names of all the things they could see in the picture followed by picture description and creative writing. All these activities help in inspiring visual thinking and building language skills. Few grammar games were also played, like Analogies. Questions were asked such as: Bracelet is to arm as ring is to (finger), Fingers are to hand as toes are to (foot), A is to Z as beginning is to (end), Eyes are to see as ears are to (hear), Classifying: Describing ways three things are similar where parents had to state what the group of things they have in common and how they are alike followed by What could you use to?, Rapid Fire and Finishing sentences by adding a rhyming word. All these were exciting and thought provoking games. The workshop concluded by sharing few tips with the parents that will enable the children to acquire language skills in fun-filled day to day conversation and activities.
